Understanding Carbon Footprints

üåç What is a Carbon Footprint?

A carbon footprint is the total amount of greenhouse gas emissions (measured in CO‚ÇÇ equivalents) caused directly or indirectly by your activities. This includes emissions from energy use, transportation, food production, and consumption of goods and services.

Global CO‚ÇÇ emissions have surpassed 36 billion tonnes annually. Rising temperatures, extreme weather events, and ecosystem disruptions threaten food security, water resources, and human settlements worldwide.

Buildings (5-10%): Residential and commercial building operations
